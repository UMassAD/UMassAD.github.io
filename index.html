Spring 2019: the team learned key scientific communication skills through delivering chalk talks and drafting technology explainers on anaerobic digestion processes. This work culminated in both the team winning 1st Place at the 2019 iCons Annual Energy Debate and a position paper summarizing the key challenges, technological breakthroughs, and implementation plan for a full-scale biodigester on-campus. This position paper formed the basis for a proposal, aiming to build a lab-scale demonstration biodigester, which was submitted to the Sustainability Innovation & Engagement Fund; funding was approved in April 2020.

Spring 2020: the team engaged in hands-on lab work building Arduino sensors and a prototype lab-scale biodigester. When the semester shifted to remote work, we turned to reproducing literature simulations for expected biomethane yields and biowaste conversion in our lab-scale biodigester. Our work for the semester culminated in a manuscript on the iCons Innovation Portal and a policy brief describing the economic benefits for the campus to implement a full-scale anaerobic digester.

Summer 2020: the team explored the techno-economics of anaerobic digestion processes. This work entailed literature reviews on the scientific, geographical, economic, and political factors impacting the economic feasibility of an anaerobic digester in different states and municipalities. With the help of iCons and Chemical Engineering Faculty, the team built a prototype version of CADE — the Calculator for Anaerobic Digestion techno-Economics — which takes in a set of economic and geography specific inputs, and outputs economic returns to an anaerobic digester investment. To develop a base case process flow diagram acting as the backbone for CADE, the team surveyed the literature to determine ideal reactor and separation processes optimized with respect to cost and Bio-Methane Potential (BMP), forming the base case process flow diagram. Currently, the team is working on refining cost and performance parameters of the base case process flow diagram using ASPEN simulations, as well as improving CADE UI for public use.

The iCons Biodigester Team was founded in 2019 by a group of iCons students interested in anaerobic digestion technologies for improving campus sustainability. As iCons students, we have been impressed upon the need to tackle society's grand challenges in renewable energy through collaboration and innovative thinking. Based on preliminary research, we believed that anaerobic digestion provided a viable pathway with which to reduce the UMass campus's carbon footprint and achieve its goal of 100% clean energy by 2030.
